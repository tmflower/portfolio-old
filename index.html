About Me

When I was a little kid, one day my dad brought home a Texas Instruments home computer.

Laugh all you want today, but this was high tech stuff at the time.
I took to it like a to water, and before long I had taught myself enough to write some simple programs and make magical things happen on that tiny, monochromatic screen.

Now, I thought this was pretty cool stuff, but no one else seemed all that impressed by my tinkering. After all, I was a little girl at a time when Barbies and Easy Bake Ovens were the activities I was expected to be interested in. Over time, I tinkered less and less, and we all know what eventually happened to that TI home computer.

Fast forward several decades, and I found myself coming full circle as I contemplated a career change. The childlike curiosity, creativity, and relentless urge to "figure it out" is still a huge part of who I am and what has led me to pursue a career in software engineering.

I'll never know what might have happened if someone had gotten hold of me when I was a child and really encouraged me to pursue my interests and abilities, but I do know that it's not too late!
